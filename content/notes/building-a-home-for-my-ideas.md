I've always had ideas.

Creative ones, business ones, product ones. Most of them stayed in my head for way too long.

I'd think about them, talk about them, and then move on to the next thing.

I wanted to see them actually exist.

I needed more structure, so I decided to treat my ideas with more respect.

That's how The Adda Labs started.

It's not a startup, it's more like a system.

A way to take ideas out of my head, test them in the real world, and see what actually works.

I also started bringing in other people with different skills and perspectives.

Now, my ideas have a clear path. They get worked on.

Some find their place quickly, some take longer to move forward.

Everything feels more tangible. There's more action and a lot more progress.
