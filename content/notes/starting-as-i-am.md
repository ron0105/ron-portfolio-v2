---
title: Starting as I am
excerpt: "I thought I had to be 'ready' before I could start. I was wrong."
date: Jan 2026
readTime: 2 min
takeaway: "Clarity comes from moving. You find your way by going forward."
---

For a long time, I thought I should be ready before starting anything.

Ready with the right skills, ready with total clarity, and ready with a perfect plan.

I spent a lot of time preparing.

I'd research, read guides, and overthink everything.

It was a learning phase, but I wasn't actually doing anything.

Then I noticed something.

Every time I actually started, I learned exactly what was needed while I was doing it.

Action brought the clarity I was looking for in my research.

The first version was just a step, but it existed.

And once something exists, you can make it better.

That's how most of my work actually got built.

I started and learned along the way.

Now I find clarity through action, not before it.

I start small, then adjust. It's a living process, and things are moving.
